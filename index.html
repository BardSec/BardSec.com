Experience

Hands-on operator with leadership range.

  • 20+ years serving K12 schools

    I’ve spent my career at the intersection of technology, security, and education. 

    That means I’ve seen what happens when well-intentioned security advice collides with real classrooms, limited staffing, aging infrastructure, and political pressure. 

    I believe cybersecurity should be boring in the best possible way. Predictable. Understandable. Grounded in fundamentals. 

    My work focuses on helping organizations improve security maturity through governance, risk management, and human-centered controls, not chasing the latest threat headline or vendor promise. 

    When security is done well, it fades into the background and lets people do their jobs safely.

  • 4+ years  advising companies that schools depend on

    I’ve worked with SaaS companies selling into the K–12 market, helping them navigate the realities of how schools evaluate, adopt, and trust technology. 

    This work focuses on route to market rather than product engineering. I advise teams on the unique operational, regulatory, and cultural constraints schools face, and how those realities should shape messaging, content, and engagement. 

    That includes developing security and privacy narratives that resonate with district leaders, creating educational content for non-technical audiences, and leading webinars that help schools understand risk without feeling sold to. 

    Because I’ve spent years inside K–12 environments, I help companies avoid common missteps. Overpromising. Using enterprise language that doesn’t translate. Treating security as a feature instead of a shared responsibility. 

    The goal is not faster sales. It’s more durable relationships built on clarity, credibility, and respect for how schools actually operate.

  • Education & Credentials    

    Graduate training in both cyber security and education, supported by CISSP, CISM, CCSP, CETL, and advanced CompTIA certifications across security, cloud, and infrastructure.
